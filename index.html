by Todd Olive, David Bogard, and Robert Moser, September 2019
Large eddy simulation of compressible, shaped-hole film cooling

The computational study shows that at typical gas turbine engine operating conditions, supersonic flow will exist in shaped film cooling holes. This leads to shock waves within the hole causing an unsteady oscillation of the coolant jet and significant degradation of the film cooling performance.

by John McClintic, Dale Fox, Fraser Jones, David Bogard, Thomas Dyson, and Zachary Weber, March 2019
Flow Physics of Diffused-Exit Film Cooling Holes Fed by Internal Crossflow

In this study measurements were made of thermal and velocity fields within and downstream a 7-7-7 shaped film cooling hole which was fed my an internal crossflow channel with varying channel velocities. These measurements provide insight to the mechanisms causing the coolant flow to skew to one side or both sides of the hole at the exit of the hole.

by Kyle Chavez, Thomas Slavens, and David Bogard, September 2017
Experimentally Measured Effects of Incidence Angle on the Adiabatic and Overall Effectiveness of a Fully Cooled Turbine Airfoil With Shaped Showerhead Holes

This is a study of the effects of the approach flow direction on the film effectiveness and overall cooling effectiveness for a fully turbine vane. This study was unique in using advanced shaped film cooling holes in the showerhead region and downstream suction and pressure sides of the vane.
